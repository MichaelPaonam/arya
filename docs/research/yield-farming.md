# Yield Farming Research

## What Is Yield Farming?

Yield farming is a DeFi investment strategy where investors stake, lend, or lock up cryptocurrency assets to earn returns. Returns come through governance rewards, transaction fees, and other crypto assets. Its primary purpose is improving liquidity in DeFi protocols.

## Core Strategies

### Liquidity Provision
- Deposit token pairs into DEX pools (e.g., Uniswap, Curve) to enable trading
- Earn a portion of trading fees generated by pool users
- Risk: impermanent loss when token prices diverge

### Staking
- Lock tokens for set or flexible periods to support blockchain networks
- Receive rewards based on stake amount and duration
- Lower risk but typically lower returns

### Lending & Borrowing
- Deposit holdings on platforms (Aave, Compound) to earn interest
- Borrowers collateralize assets to obtain loans for additional yield
- Risk: liquidation if collateral value drops

### Liquidity Mining
- Earn platform-native tokens by depositing liquidity into specific pools
- Tokens often provide governance rights
- Risk: token value can decrease, reducing effective yield

## 2026 Trends

### Delta-Neutral Stablecoin Farming
- Deposit stablecoins (USDC) into lending platforms or LPs
- Hedge via derivatives short positions
- Returns: mid-single to low double-digit annual yields
- Risks: funding reversals, execution errors, platform issues

### Restaking and Layered Yield
- Stake base assets (ETH) → receive liquid staking tokens
- Redeploy tokens across multiple protocols simultaneously
- Income sources: staking rewards + restaking incentives + liquidity fees
- Risks: slashing penalties, rehypothecation exposure

### Yield Tokenization (Pendle)
- Split yield-bearing assets into principal and yield components
- Enables fixed-rate positioning or yield speculation
- Requires understanding pricing dynamics and maturity schedules

## Industry Shift
- Away from unsustainable triple-digit APYs driven by token incentives
- Toward "structured, risk-adjusted income" - "yield engineering"
- Focus on efficiency and predictability over chasing highest returns
- Capital reuse across multiple protocols becoming standard

## Key Risks

| Risk | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Impermanent Loss | Loss from price divergence between paired tokens in LP pools |
| Smart Contract Vulnerabilities | Bugs in protocol code can lead to fund loss |
| Liquidation | Collateral drops below threshold in leveraged positions |
| Market Volatility | Sudden price movements affect all DeFi positions |
| Rug Pulls | Malicious projects designed to steal deposited funds |
| Regulatory | Evolving legal landscape for DeFi protocols |

## Major Protocols

- **Uniswap** - Leading DEX, concentrated liquidity (v3/v4)
- **Aave** - Lending/borrowing protocol, flash loans
- **Compound** - Lending protocol, governance tokens
- **Curve Finance** - Stablecoin-focused DEX, low slippage
- **Pendle** - Yield tokenization and trading
- **Lido** - Liquid staking for ETH
- **Yearn Finance** - Yield optimization vaults
